This section captures the proof-of-concept work for the **Brac** engagement —
what we're proving, where it runs, who owns which piece, and the milestones
the POC has to clear before it can graduate to a production rollout.

## What the POC is for

A POC is not a pilot. The goal is to *prove* a small number of load-bearing
assumptions in a contained environment, with a written go/no-go decision at
the end. Anything outside that scope is explicitly out of bounds for this
phase.

## Pages in this section

The remaining pages cover, in order:

1. **Scope and success criteria** — what we're proving, what we're not.
2. **Environment and topology** — the cluster, the network, the identity
   provider, and how the POC sandbox connects (or doesn't) to the rest of
   the platform.
3. **Workloads under test** — the apps or pipelines exercised end-to-end.
4. **Operational handoff** — who runs what during the POC and how
   findings are recorded.
5. **Decision log** — the running list of go/no-go calls and their
   rationale.
